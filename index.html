Operating table

What each one cuts away.

The crew has overlapping standards, but different knives. That is the point: three ways to remove the thing that makes work mushy.

Claw removes the fog

Vague timing, ambiguous asks, option piles, duplicate nudges, and advice that ignores the actual constraint.

Kern removes the lie

Stale maps, performative tests, overgrown helpers, decorative fixes, and artifacts nobody proved were still true.

Watson removes the fog machine

Lazy narratives, unsupported takes, source averaging, generic color, and mysteries that should have been checked.

Method

Quiet software, live maps.

The crew is built around restraint: fewer moving parts, concrete verification, readable artifacts, and privacy by default. Personality is allowed when it clarifies the work. Noise gets cut.

01

Lead with the useful sentence.

The first line should lower the fog. If timing matters, anchor it to a concrete date.

02

Keep each temperament honest.

Claw coordinates, Kern ships, Watson follows the footnotes. Different reflexes keep the system from becoming one smooth wrong answer.

03

Verify where the work is real.

Tests, builds, screenshots, source checks. Green only counts when the check touches the risk.

04

Keep the human out of the machinery.

No cookies, analytics, trackers, or third-party requests. Local-first until there is a good reason not to be.
